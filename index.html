Here's my story

“Wind howled through the night, carrying a scent that would change the world.”

Hounding first sentence, huh? Christopher Paolini wrote that – the first sentence of his first novel, Eragon, which was published when he was 15. I found Eragon at a book fair when I was 12, and that’s when I decided if he could do it, I could too. My ambition as a writer was born.

I read voraciously my whole life—my mother was a librarian so we had no shortage of books. No shopping trip was complete without a stop at either Barnes & Noble or the library. My summers were marked by races to see how many books I could read in a month (my record was something like 22.) I learned to appreciate what a story could do for me—take me out of myself—and teach me something in the process.

Fast forward to 2010, when my high school adviser first suggested I join the newspaper staff. I was hesitant at first; I liked creative writing, and news sounded dull. But I joined anyway, and discovered that my love for telling stories applied beautifully. By 2012 I was editor-in-chief of The Terrapin Times, and I knew that it was journalism I wanted to pursue. Creative writing became a side project as I pursued news and feature writing.

After high school I interned with a local paper, The Osprey Observer, where I had 10 articles published. And now I’m halfway through my journalism journey at the University of Florida, working on all a matter of skills so I’ll be ready to tackle the world when I graduate.

Eight years and three well-loved copies of Eragon later, something amazing happened. As I was looking up local conventions for the season, I stumbled across a tiny one near USF. And the guest of honor was none other than Christopher Paolini himself. Fate was kind to me, and on October 19, 2013 I got to march right up to my idol and thank him for inspiring me all those years ago. He blessed me in the ancient language of Eragon and encouraged me to continue with my writing dreams.

I am determined to do just that.
